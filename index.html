Hi! I am Sutirtha, currently a Physics major at Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR), with research interests in gravitation and cosmology, theoretical astrophysics, and dark matter phenomenology. My broader motivation is to understand the nature and history of the universe, from the smallest to the largest scales. My current research is to construct the local dark matter phase space from a data-driven Bayesian inference of baryonic matter, the direct and indirect detection of “dark matter,” and its associated near-field cosmological implications.

My academic background helped me to gain a deeper understanding of both theoretical physics and data science and computational methods. I have explored a variety of subfields on problems related to observational cosmology, large-scale structure formation, galaxy evolution, radio astronomy, and astrophysical data analysis. I use Bayesian statistical inference, machine learning, and numerical methods to study large-scale datasets. I am also interested in understanding conformal field theory (CFT) and AdSd+1/CFTd duality and aspects of perturbative string theory.

I am open to collaborating with researchers and academic groups on projects of mutual interest. Please feel free to reach out to discuss potential problem statements. Outside of academics, I pursue the arts and music as my hobbies; I hold a diploma in fine arts, play the violin, and am currently learning the flute.

Visiting Scholar & Physics Major
Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR)
Dec. 2024 - Sep. 2025 • Mumbai, India
Supervisor: Prof. Subha Majumdar. Constraining the local dark matter density using stellar kinematics and Milky Way rotation curves from Gaia DR3, DESI, SDSS, and LAMOST data extending to 1 Mpc. Research Intern - MADMAX
Max Planck Institute for Physics
Apr. 2024 - Present • Munich, Germany
Supervisor: Prof. Allen Caldwell; Collaboration: DESY-CERN. Analyzing signal extraction from three-component composite power spectra (S+N+B) in the Magnetized Disc Axion Search Experiment dielectric haloscope, with roughly 80 high-index disks and a 10 T magnet boosting axion-induced microwave signals from the 10^-27 W scale toward the 10^-23 W scale. Applied Savitzky-Golay filtering for background removal, systematically varied polynomial order, window length, and Weibull signal parameters, and used Bayesian MCMC with BAT.jl plus Spearman and Kolmogorov tests to quantify p-value bias and axion detectability under realistic conditions.
